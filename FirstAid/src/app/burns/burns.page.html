Burns

The universal rule for burning by fire is to cease the source of the flames;
then use cold water to cool the affected area.

This is the indicated output, since there are no medication, product or homemade recipe to reduce the pain caused by a burn or improve the injury.

The famous ointments, even those available in pharmacies, stress still the skin cells, irritating it and causing infections.

If bubbles appear, do not blow them at all:

they are natural protection against burns.

Bubbles are typical of second degree burns, deeper, and should only be handled by a physician.

Avoid:

wrapping the lesion with tissues or cotton they can stick.

Accessories such as watches, bracelets, rings, laces and earrings, on the other hand, should be withdrawn.

This is because the human body tends to expand after suffering a burn and these objects can get stuck.
