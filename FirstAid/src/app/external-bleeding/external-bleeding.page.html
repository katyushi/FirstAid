External Bleeding

What to do:
  • Lay the victim horizontally.

  • Apply a sterile dressing on the wound or, if it is not present,
    a washed cloth, exerting a firm pressure with one or both hands,
    with a finger or even with a clean bandage,
    depending on the location and extent of the injury.

  • If it is saturated with blood, put another on top of it, but without removing the first.

  • Make the compression last until the bleeding stops (at least 10 minutes).

  • If the bleeding stops, apply a compressive dressing over the wound.

Caution: the rescuer must wear disposable gloves before any procedure.

If it is a member wound with heavy bleeding it may be necessary to apply a garrote tourniquet.

The garrote may be rubber or improvised with a narrow strip of cloth or a tie.

How to apply a garrote tourniquet
It is a serious situation that requires urgent transportation to the Hospital.
