External Bleeding

What to do:

- Lay the victim horizontally.

- Apply a sterile dressing on the wound or, if it is not present, a washed cloth, exerting a firm pressure with one or both hands, with a finger or even with a clean bandage, depending on the location and extent of the injury. If it is saturated with blood, put another on top of it, but without removing the first.

- Make the compression last until the bleeding stops (at least 10 minutes).

- If the bleeding stops, apply a compressive dressing over the wound.

Caution: the rescuer must wear disposable gloves before any procedure.
