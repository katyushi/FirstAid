Internal Bleeding

Internal bleeding should always be suspected when blood is not seen to flow but the victim has one or more of the following signs and symptoms:

Signals and Symptons:

- Thirst.

- Feeling of cold (chills).

- Progressively faster and weaker pulse.

In even more serious cases:

- Paleness.

- Cooling, especially of the ends

- Buzzing.

- Change in the state of consciousness.

What you should do:

- Calm the victim and keep her awake.

- Unscrew the clothes.

- Keep the victim comfortably warm.

- Put it in Safety Lateral Position.

It is a serious situation that requires urgent transportation to the hospital.

What you should not do:

- Give drink or food

Caution: the rescuer must wear disposable gloves before any procedure.
